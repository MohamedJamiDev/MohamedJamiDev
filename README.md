<summary>📚 <strong>My Journey in Tech</strong></summary>

I started my technology journey in a place where **technology didn't exist**—a small village in **Sudan**, surrounded by farms and livestock. **Electricity was a luxury**, and computers were unheard of.

At **13 years old**, life took a dramatic turn when I migrated to the **United States as a refugee**, searching not just for safety but for **opportunity**.

The **cultural and technological gap** was overwhelming. While my peers navigated computers, phones, and gaming consoles with ease, I was **starting from zero**. I faced **language barriers, bullying, and self-doubt**. But instead of letting these challenges hold me back, I used them as fuel to push forward.

### **🔹 From Manufacturing to Tech**
My journey through **manufacturing at Applied Materials** taught me invaluable skills that directly translate to software development:

- **Process Analysis:** As a Manufacturing Technician, I learned to break down complex assembly processes into smaller, manageable steps—a skill that now helps me in **debugging code** and **designing test cases**.
- **Problem-Solving in Action:** I developed a **systematic approach to troubleshooting** by:
  - Identifying faulty semiconductor components through precise testing procedures
  - Implementing process improvements that reduced assembly time by 25%
  - Creating detailed documentation that helped streamline our workflow
- **Quality Assurance Mindset:** Manufacturing taught me that **quality isn't an afterthought**—it's built into every step of the process. This perspective now shapes how I approach **software testing and development**.

### **💡 Unique Perspective in Tech**
My non-traditional background gives me distinct advantages:
- **Fresh Eyes:** Starting from zero means I question assumptions and find innovative solutions others might miss
- **Resilience:** Overcoming language and cultural barriers taught me to persist through debugging challenges
- **User Empathy:** My experience learning technology from scratch helps me understand and design for users of all skill levels

### **🔹 The Bridge to Software Development**
The transition from hardware to software was natural:
- Manufacturing taught me to think in **systems and processes**
- Testing components prepared me for **software testing methodologies**
- Documentation experience translated to **clean, well-commented code**
- Team collaboration skills ready me for **agile development environments**

Today, I'm leveraging these experiences as I build **test automation frameworks** and develop **backend applications**. Each line of code I write carries the precision of manufacturing, the curiosity of a newcomer, and the determination of someone who started from scratch.

**Every challenge I face in tech reminds me: starting from zero isn't a disadvantage—it's my superpower.** It gives me a unique perspective that combines manufacturing precision with innovative problem-solving, turning every obstacle into an opportunity for growth.
